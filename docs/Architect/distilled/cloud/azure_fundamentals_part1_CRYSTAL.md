## Key Concepts

*   **Cloud Computing Definition:** Delivery of computing services (servers, storage, databases, networking, software, analytics, and intelligence) over the Internet ("the cloud") to offer faster innovation, flexible resources, and economies of scale.
*   **Shared Responsibility Model:** A distribution of responsibilities between the cloud provider (Microsoft Azure in this case) and the customer. The provider handles physical infrastructure, while the customer manages data and security within their cloud environment.
*   **Cloud Deployment Models:**
    *   **Public Cloud:** Services offered over the public internet, owned and operated by a third-party provider (e.g., Azure).
    *   **Private Cloud:** Infrastructure dedicated to a single organization, either hosted on-premises or by a third-party.
    *   **Hybrid Cloud:** A combination of public and private clouds, allowing for data and application portability between them.
*   **Consumption-Based Pricing:** A pricing model where customers pay only for the resources they consume.
*   **Microsoft Azure:** A comprehensive cloud computing platform offering a wide range of services.
